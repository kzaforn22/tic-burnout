Theoretical Framework

This project integrates two frameworks to explain why TIC may reduce burnout in juvenile justice settings:

Occupational Health Psychology

Focuses on employee well-being, job demands, resources, leadership, and organizational climate. In high-stress public-safety contexts, interventions that add resources (support, clarity, psychological safety) can buffer demands and reduce burnout risk.

Trauma-Informed Organizational Theory

Treats trauma exposure as a systems issue. TIC reshapes practice norms toward safety, transparency, empowerment, and collaboration. These mechanisms can reduce depersonalization and increase professional efficacy.

Integrated model: TIC as a mediator between trauma exposure and burnout outcomes.
