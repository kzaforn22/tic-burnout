Research Questions & Hypotheses

Primary question: Can participation in trauma-informed care (TIC) training reduce burnout among juvenile justice professionals?

Secondary question: How might TIC function as an organizational protective factor against secondary traumatic stress?

Hypothesis (proposed): Professionals who have completed TIC training will report lower burnout (emotional exhaustion & depersonalization) and higher personal accomplishment compared to professionals without training, measured using the Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) subscales.

Why this question matters

  • Workforce sustainability: burnout is linked to turnover intentions and reduced morale.
  • Service quality: staff well-being shapes the consistency and relational climate youth experience.
  • System-level leverage: TIC is designed to change organizational practice, not just individual coping.

Note: The MBI is proprietary; a real-world implementation should use it via authorized access or use validated open instruments where appropriate.
