Overview

Juvenile justice professionals operate in high-demand environments where chronic exposure to youth trauma, crisis events, and organizational strain can increase risk for burnout. This project presents an evidence-informed proposal that frames trauma-informed care (TIC) training as a system-level intervention to protect staff well-being and improve organizational performance.

Problem
Burnout + secondary traumatic stress undermine retention, safety, and service quality.
Intervention
TIC training & practices as an organizational mediator (psychological safety & resilience).
Approach
Literature synthesis + public workforce datasets + proposed quasi-experimental evaluation.
Conceptual model: Trauma exposure → TIC training/practices → resilience mechanisms → burnout outcomes (MBI subscales). (This is a framework figure, not empirical results.)
Transparency. This capstone is a proposal (hypothetical study). Public datasets are used for context and leadership insight, and any “illustrative” figures are clearly labeled as conceptual.
